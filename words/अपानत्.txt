Pur.</i> (2. 10. 16.) अनु प्राणन्ति यं प्राणाः प्राणन्तं सर्व- जन्तुषु । अपानन्तमपानन्ति नरदेवमिवानुगाः, it deserves notice that <i>Srīdharasvāmin</i> takes the word अपानत् in the sense ‘ceasing to perform vital functions’, viz. ‘they (according to the comm., the senses) cease to exercise their functions when it (the vital air प्राण) ceases to perform its function’: प्राणा इन्द्रियाणि यं (scil. प्राणं, the word of the preceding verse) प्राणन्तं चेष्टां कुर्वन्तमनु पश्चात्प्राणन्ति चेष्टां कुर्वन्ति । अपानन्तं चेष्टां त्यजन्तमनु अपानन्ति चेष्टां त्यजन्ति । राजानमनु भृत्या इव; but it seems very doubtful whether he is correct in this construction of the verse, as प्राणाः might be taken in the general sense ‘vital airs’, and the sense ‘they perform the function of exhaling and inhaling according to the function of life (प्राण)’ would be in conformity with the usual meaning of the words in question.
