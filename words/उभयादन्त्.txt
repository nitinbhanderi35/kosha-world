The distinction occurs in a late hymn of the Rigveda,<sup>1</sup> and is several times alluded to in the later Saṃhitās<sup>2</sup> and the Brāhmaṇas.<sup>3</sup> In one passage of the Taittirīya Saṃhitā<sup>4</sup> man is classed with the horse as <i>ubhayā-dant.</i> The opposite is <i>anyato-dant,</i> ‘having incisors in one jaw only,’ a term regularly applied to cattle,<sup>5</sup> the eight incisors of which are, in fact, limited to the lower jaw.
